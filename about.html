Park History

Opening Day

The park was overwhelmed by guests. Thanks to counterfeit tickets, almost twice as many guests as expected entered the park that day.

Disneyland opened on July 17th, 1955. From the very first day of operation, it was incredibly popular, and it was easy to see why. The first of park of its kind in United states, Disneyland was a unique entertainment experience for both children and adults. Prior to Disneyland, anything that might be refered to as an amusement park was either places that were considered too seedy or dangerous to take children, or were places for the children to have all the fun while the parents sat and watched. It was at once such place, Griffith Park in Los Angeles California, while sitting on a park bench as his daughters rode a merry-go-round, that Walt Disney first envisioned the park that would become Disneyland. He wanted a place where children and parents could have fun together. Construction began on July 16th, 1954. Yes, you read that correctly. Disneyland was build in just one year and one day.

Over the past 66 years, Disneyland has become one of the most popular theme parks in the world. Second only to it's sister park, Magic Kingdom at the Walt Disney World Resort, it hosts almost 20 million guests annually. Though the park has undergone significant changes since opening day, it still maintains all of the magic and delight that helped to make it "The Happiest Place On Earth"
